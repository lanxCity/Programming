The course is aimed at providing avenue for students in the Nigerian Universities to acquire industrial skills and experience in their course of study, and prepare the students for real life work situations in which they are likely to meet after graduation. This course, in University of Ibadan, is being coordinated and supervised by the Industrial Training Coordinating Centre (ITCC). It provides avenue for students to acquire industrial skills and experience in their variety course of study. And it also provides students with an opportunity to apply their theoretical knowledge in real life work situation, thereby helping them to bridge the gap between the practical and theoretical knowledge of their disciplines.
Above-ground biomass includes all biomass in living vegetation, both woody and herbaceous, above the soil including stems, stumps, branches, bark, seeds and foliage. Above-ground biomass is the most visible of all the carbon pools, and changes in it are an important indicator of change or of the impact of an intervention on benefits related to both carbon mitigation and other matters. Above-ground biomass is a key pool for most land-based projects. Among all, forest is the largest pool of the above-ground biomass. Nature has provided us with natural carbon “sinks” or “sponges” like the terrestrial ecosystem and the oceans. Forest’s ecosystem is one of the most important carbon sinks of the terrestrial ecosystem. Forest’s vegetation takes up the carbon dioxide in the process of photosynthesis. Forest plantations are defined as those forest stands established by planting or/and by seeding in the process of afforestation or reforestation. In other word, forest plantations are a type of managed forest in which trees are planted (as opposed to naturally regenerated), of the same age and generally of the same species, and intended to maximize the production of fiber.
