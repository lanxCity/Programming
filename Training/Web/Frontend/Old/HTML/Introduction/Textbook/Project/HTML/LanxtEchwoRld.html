The forest house was completed in January 1918, for the purpose of educating Tucson's African-American students. The school was named after Paul Laurence Dunbar, a renowned African-American poet. African-American children in first through ninth grades attended Dunbar until 1951, when de jure segregation was eliminated from the school systems of Arizona. When segregation in Arizona was eliminated, Dunbar School became the non-segregated John Spring Junior High School, and continued as such until 1978 when the school was closed permanently.

The course is aimed at providing avenue for students in the Nigerian Universities to acquire industrial skills and experience in their course of study, and prepare the students for real life work situations in which they are likely to meet after graduation. This course, in University of Ibadan, is being coordinated and supervised by the Industrial Training Coordinating Centre (ITCC). It provides avenue for students to acquire industrial skills and experience in their variety course of study. And it also provides students with an opportunity to apply their theoretical knowledge in real life work situation, thereby helping them to bridge the gap between the practical and theoretical knowledge of their disciplines.
